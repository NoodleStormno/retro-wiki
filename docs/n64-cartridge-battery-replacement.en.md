# Nintendo 64 Cartridge Battery Replacement Complete Guide (Without Losing Saves)

![Nintendo 64](images/n64.webp)

> Your Zelda: Ocarina of Time save file disappeared again? All GoldenEye 007 unlocks gone? N64 cartridge CR2032 batteries last about 5-10 years, and when they die, all your saves "evaporate." But don't despair! There's a way to replace the battery without losing precious save data. Master this technique, and your gaming memories will live forever!

## Required Tools
- 3.8mm game bit screwdriver (essential!)
- Soldering iron, 25-40W, fine tip
- Solder wire and flux
- Desoldering pump or solder wick
- Tweezers, fine point
- External power source: 3V battery holder or adjustable power supply
- Wires and clips (alligator clips)
- Multimeter for voltage testing
- Anti-static mat and workbench
- New CR2032 battery (Panasonic or Sony brand recommended)

## Repair Steps

### 1. Preparation (Most Critical!)
1. **Confirm dead battery**: Test battery voltage with multimeter (below 2.5V needs replacement)
2. **Prepare external power**: 3V DC power source, correct polarity
3. **Test external power**: Ensure stable 3V output
4. **Gather all tools**: Get everything ready to avoid leaving midway
5. **Clean workspace**: Avoid dust contamination
6. **Mental preparation**: Requires patience and steady hands

### 2. Safe Cartridge Disassembly
1. Use 3.8mm game bit screwdriver to remove bottom screws
2. Gently separate housing with plastic spudger (watch for 4 clips)
3. Remove circuit board, place on anti-static mat
4. **Don't disconnect anything**: Keep circuit board intact
5. Locate battery: Usually in center or edge of board

### 3. Connect External Power (Key to Saving Saves!)
1. Use multimeter to confirm battery positive/negative terminals
2. Connect external power positive to battery positive solder point
3. Connect external power negative to battery negative solder point
4. **Important**: Attach negative first, then positive
5. Ensure secure connections that won't accidentally disconnect
6. Verify with multimeter: Board should have 3V voltage

### 4. Solder Battery Replacement
1. Heat battery positive solder point with soldering iron
2. Gently remove old battery with tweezers (external power stays connected)
3. Clean positive pad, remove old solder
4. Repeat for negative terminal, clean pad
5. **Keep external power connected**: External power supplies SRAM during this process

### 5. Install New Battery
1. Prepare new CR2032 battery, confirm polarity
2. Place new battery in correct position
3. Solder negative terminal first: Quick and precise, avoid overheating
4. Solder positive terminal: Complete quickly
5. **Keep external power connected while soldering**
6. Check solder joints: Should be smooth and shiny, no cold joints

### 6. Disconnect External Power & Testing
1. Disconnect external power positive first
2. Disconnect external power negative
3. Test new battery voltage with multimeter: Should be around 3V
4. Test saves: Temporarily assemble cartridge, insert into console
5. Confirm all save files intact
6. Fully reassemble cartridge, tighten screws
