# Nintendo 64 Motherboard Replacement Complete Guide

![Nintendo 64](images/n64.webp)

> Your N64 completely "brain dead"? No power response, power light off, or completely black screen. Could be key chips burned on motherboard, capacitors exploded, or circuit board corrosion. Don't throw away yet! N64 motherboard replacement is actually simpler than imagined, just find suitable replacement board, your console can be reborn.

## Required Tools
- Cross-head screwdriver, 4.5mm console-specific
- Plastic spudger, avoid scratching case
- Anti-static wrist strap, protect motherboard components
- Multimeter, detect voltage and continuity
- Hot air station or soldering iron (optional, for desoldering)
- Magnifying glass, inspect solder joints and components
- Tweezers, handle small parts

## Repair Steps

### 1. Pre-operation Diagnosis: Confirm Motherboard Issue
1. Check power supply: Use multimeter to measure power board output (should have 3.3V and 12V)
2. Check indicator light: Power light off may be motherboard issue
3. Check video output: No signal may be video chip issue
4. **Important**: Confirm motherboard issue not other component failure

### 2. Complete N64 Console Disassembly
1. Remove 6 screws from bottom (note: 2 under rubber feet)
2. Carefully separate top/bottom shells, connected by clips
3. Disconnect power board from motherboard cable
4. Remove motherboard mounting screws (4 screws)
5. Remove old motherboard, note ribbon cable connections

### 3. Prepare Replacement Motherboard
1. **Model matching**: Confirm replacement motherboard model (NUS-001/USA, NUS-001/JPN, etc.)
2. Inspect replacement board: Visual check for obvious damage
3. Clean edge connectors: Use isopropyl alcohol to clean all connectors
4. Test replacement board (if possible): Test at seller or quick power test

### 4. Install New Motherboard
1. Align screw holes, gently place into case
2. Connect power board ribbon cable (note orientation)
3. Connect video/audio output board ribbon cable
4. Connect controller interface board ribbon cable
5. Secure motherboard screws (don't overtighten)

### 5. Assembly and Testing
1. Close case, tighten all screws
2. Connect power and video cables
3. **First power test**: Observe if power light illuminates
4. Insert game cartridge, test game operation
5. Test all 4 controller ports
6. Continuous operation 1 hour, test stability
