# PSP Go Battery Replacement - Complete Guide

## Overview

PSP Go's built-in lithium battery capacity decreases over time, reducing runtime. This guide details safe PSP Go battery replacement to restore device battery life.

## Required Tools

- Phillips screwdriver (PH00)
- Plastic spudger
- Tweezers
- New PSP Go specific battery (model: PSP-S110)
- Anti-static wrist strap (recommended)

## Safety Warnings

⚠️ **Battery Safety Critical** ⚠️

1. **No piercing**: Never puncture or damage lithium batteries
2. **Avoid shorts**: Don't let battery contacts touch metal objects
3. **Heat avoidance**: Don't operate in high temperature environments
4. **Professional disposal**: Damaged batteries require professional recycling
5. **Use genuine**: Only use OEM or certified safe batteries

## Battery Specifications

- **Model**: PSP-S110
- **Type**: Lithium-ion polymer
- **Voltage**: 3.7V
- **Capacity**: 930mAh
- **Dimensions**: ~53×33×5mm

## Detailed Steps

### Step 1: Safety Preparation

1. Completely power off PSP Go
2. Disconnect all external devices
3. Place insulating mat on work surface
4. Wear anti-static wrist strap (if available)

### Step 2: Remove Back Cover

1. Place PSP Go face down
2. Remove 4 crosshead screws from back cover
3. Use plastic spudger to pry open cover from bottom
4. Carefully remove back cover, avoid damaging clips

### Step 3: Disconnect Old Battery

1. Locate battery connector (right side of motherboard)
2. Gently lift battery connector with plastic tool
3. Carefully remove battery ribbon cable
4. **Important**: Ensure battery completely disconnected

### Step 4: Remove Old Battery

1. Battery secured with adhesive to chassis
2. Gently lift battery corner with plastic spudger
3. Slowly separate battery from adhesive
4. If adhesive stubborn, use minimal isopropyl alcohol
5. Completely remove old battery

### Step 5: Clean Battery Compartment

1. Clean residual adhesive with cotton swabs
2. Wipe contact areas with isopropyl alcohol
3. Ensure compartment completely dry
4. Check battery contacts cleanliness

### Step 6: Install New Battery

1. Apply new adhesive to new battery back
2. Align battery ribbon with connector
3. Gently insert ribbon, ensure fully seated
4. Place battery in compartment, press to secure
5. Ensure battery flat, no bulging

### Step 7: Reassemble

1. Replace back cover, align all clips
2. Press gently, ensure all clips engaged
3. Replace 4 screws
4. Don't overtighten screws

## Initial Use & Calibration

### Initial Charging
1. Use OEM charger
2. First charge: recommend 4 hours full charge
3. Don't use device while charging

### Battery Calibration
1. After full charge, use until auto-shutdown
2. Recharge fully (without interruption)
3. Repeat 2-3 times for optimal battery performance

## Performance Testing

### Runtime Testing
1. Video playback at medium brightness: ~3-4 hours
2. Gaming time: ~3-5 hours (game dependent)
3. Standby time: ~10-15 days

### Charging Testing
1. Full charge time: ~2-3 hours
2. Device temperature while charging: slightly warm normal
3. Charge indicator: orange charging, green fully charged

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Issue 1: Won't Power On
- **Possible cause**: Battery not connected or no charge
- **Solution**: Check battery connection, charge 30 minutes then retry

### Issue 2: Charge Light Not On
- **Possible cause**: Charger failure or battery connection issue
- **Solution**: Try different charger, check battery connection

### Issue 3: Swollen Battery
- **Stop using immediately**: Swollen batteries risk explosion
- **Safe handling**: Place battery in fireproof bag, contact professional recycling
- **Don't attempt repair**: Swollen batteries must be replaced

### Issue 4: Short Runtime
- **Possible cause**: Battery aging or system settings
- **Solution**: Perform battery calibration, adjust screen brightness etc.

## Battery Maintenance Guide

### Daily Use
1. **Avoid deep discharge**: Charge above 20% when possible
2. **Avoid overcharging**: Unplug after full charge
3. **Temperature range**: Use and charge at 0-35°C (32-95°F)

### Long-Term Storage
1. **Charge state**: Store at ~50% charge
2. **Storage environment**: Cool, dry place, avoid heat
3. **Regular checks**: Check charge every 3 months

### Performance Optimization
1. **Screen brightness**: Lower brightness significantly extends runtime
2. **Wireless functions**: Turn off unused Wi-Fi and Bluetooth
3. **Background apps**: Close unnecessary background applications

## Genuine vs Counterfeit Battery Identification

### Genuine Battery Characteristics
- Clear SONY logo and model number
- Clean solder joints
- Accurate weight (~20g)
- Proper packaging and anti-counterfeit labels

### Counterfeit Battery Risks
- Inflated capacity claims
- Missing safety protections
- Short lifespan
- Safety hazards

## Professional Repair Advice

### When Replacement Needed
1. Significantly reduced runtime (<2 hours)
2. Quick discharge after charging
3. Battery swelling or deformation
4. Device auto-shutdown

### Repair Considerations
1. **Purchase source**: Buy from authorized dealers
2. **Post-installation check**: Comprehensive testing after installation
3. **Old battery disposal**: Take to e-waste recycling point
4. **Warranty preservation**: Keep purchase receipt and warranty card
